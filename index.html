About

What I actually do

I've spent more than ten years at BMW, mostly turning hard technical things into experiences people enjoy using. Today I lead the UI/UX practice behind BMW's Intelligent Personal Assistant, the personalization layer (BMW ID), and the My BMW App / My MINI App.

My team recently launched the assistant's new generative-AI version, built on Amazon's Alexa+, in the all-new iX3. It swaps stiff voice commands for real conversation, in German and English. The craft behind that is the part I love: how the assistant thinks, what it sounds like, where its guardrails sit, even which voices it speaks with.

Before the AI work I spent years in mobility and car sharing — DriveNow, fleet products, the kind of projects where shaving an interaction from 50 seconds to 4 genuinely changes how people feel about a car. I also spent a good chunk of that time managing the partners behind the tech, including Amazon and Cerence, and the messy reality of getting big programmes shipped.

Currently building

An AI agent, built mostly by talking to my computer

On the side I'm vibe coding a 100% AI, chat-based app: an agent designed to handle a whole range of use cases. It's still early, so I can't share the name yet.

Building it myself — with a lot of help from Claude — has taught me more about how agentic AI really behaves than any deck ever could. It's also a good reminder that the gap between "demo" and "something people trust" is mostly design.
